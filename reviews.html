REVIEWS / SUMMARY

Asphalt 9: Legends

Asphalt 9: Legends is undeniably a pleasant affair that is used as a model for the new mobile apps rightly. It's a total stunner, boasting the effects of next-level particles, lighting and natural environment.

GrandChase DC

Dimensional Chaser is a great sequel to a great game, which managed to simultaneously adapt the gameplay elements to the mobile version and to keep everything which made the original game so good.

Call of Duty Mobile

Call of Duty: Mobile has the same flow and cadence of combat as its counterparts on consoles and the PC. The 5v5 matches are brief, however, typically lasting a few minutes due to the tighter kill and point tallies. This is a game designed for shorter sessions.

Mobile Legends

"I have been playing for three years now and for some reason every time i play it just seems not only do i get bad team mates but the lag is the worst in this game especially when it comes being epic rank on rank mode. I will do good for bit then, lag happens and throws me off. I have really good internet too." (Chris, 2019)

Clash of Clans

The game is set in a fantasy-themed persistent world where the player is a chief of a village. Clash of Clans tasks players to build their own town using the resources gained from attacking other players through the game's fighting features.

Fortnite

A global phenomenon built on a keen understanding of what makes games such great escapism, seemingly with no limit to Epic’s imagination. The only bad thing here is it has only one map and it's hard to keep up in the game.

NBA 2K Series

The size of the game has been a gift and a curse for the series in the past, and that will be the case again this year. That doesn’t mean NBA 2K20 is bad. It just means the imperfections mostly come from pushing the envelope.

Arena of Valor

Arena of Valor is best described as "League of Legends mobile," with many similar character designs. The one thing users believe could have been done better is Guild Management.

PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds Mobile

PUBG Mobile is fun, but it’s not as tense as its PC counterpart. The stakes feel lower, and it misses out on a lot of what makes the PC version of the game so special. In essence, it feels a little hollow.

Regular gameplay ran fine most of the time. There were almost always serious frame rate drops when parachuting down to the island, but that’s not totally shocking.
